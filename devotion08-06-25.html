Devotion for the Week of 8th June 2025

Month’s Theme: You Are Called Now

God’s not waiting for you to grow up or be perfect. He’s calling you to live with purpose and faith right now — in school, with friends, and in your heart.

This Week’s Focus: God Can Use You Even When You Feel Small

You might feel too young or not ready, but God can do big things through small people who trust Him.

Monthly Memory Verse

“For I know the plans I have for you,” declares the Lord,
“plans to prosper you and not to harm you, plans to give you hope and a future.”
— Jeremiah 29:11 (NIV)

God, please help us remember Your word,
to trust Your plans, and to hold onto hope every day.

This Week’s Bible Passage

“David said to Saul, ‘Let no one lose heart on account of this Philistine; your servant will go and fight him.’”
— 1 Samuel 17:32 (NIV)

Devotion

Have you ever felt too small to make a difference? Maybe you’ve thought, “I’m too young,” or “I don’t know enough,” or even “No one will listen to me.” Guess what? You’re not alone. Even the heroes in the Bible felt that way sometimes — but God still used them in amazing ways.

David was just a young shepherd boy when he showed up at the battlefield. Everyone else saw a giant (literally), but David saw something different: an opportunity to trust God. He wasn’t the strongest, or the tallest, or the most experienced warrior — but he was willing.

David had confidence not in himself, but in God. He remembered how God had helped him before — with lions and bears — and he believed God would help him again. He stepped forward when everyone else was too afraid.

Sometimes, God calls you to do something that feels way too big — like standing up for someone, speaking truth when it’s hard, or showing kindness when no one else is. You might feel small, but God is with you. And when He’s with you, small is more than enough.

You don’t need to be perfect or powerful to make a difference. You just need to trust that God is big enough to use someone like you. And He is.

Reflective Question

What’s one way you can step out in faith this week, even if you feel small or unsure?
